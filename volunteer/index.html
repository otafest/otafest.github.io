Volunteer Application

Otafest would be literally impossible without our wonderful volunteers, and we endeavor to make it as fun as possible to volunteer with us! Volunteering is a great way to level up your Otafest involvement while earning valuable experience to put on a resume and gain community service hours!

Whether you’re volunteering alone or with friends, we’ll do our best to keep you smiling throughout the whole weekend.

This page will provide you with information on volunteering with Otafest including descriptions of the various positions, benefits, responsibilities, and important dates. Please review all of the information before applying for a volunteer position.

Selections will be made after the volunteer application deadline has passed. We will notify all applicants as to whether they’ve been accepted or not at that time.

Requirements

Otafest selects its volunteers based on your submitted availability and departmental needs, in addition to the following requirements:

  • A minimum commitment of three 3-hour shifts (9 hours total).
  • Completion of a mandatory online training module.
  • Optional (but highly recommended) in-person orientation. Dates TBD.
  • You must be at least 13 years old by May 10, 2024 to volunteer for Otafest 2024.
  • Signing Otafest's volunteer contract, which outlines your responsibilities to Otafest as well as Otafest’s commitment to you. Volunteers under 18 years of age must have a parent or legal guardian sign the contract as well.

If any of these requirements are not met, we regretfully cannot host you as a volunteer this year.

Fees & Benefits

There is a non-refundable $20.00 fee to volunteer for Otafest. This fee covers your admission to Otafest, helps us ensure your commitment to volunteering, and covers some up-front administration costs involved with setting up and running Otafest. In exchange, you’ll receive the following benefits:

  • Admission to all days of the festival regardless of your volunteer schedule,
  • Access to the volunteer room throughout the festival days (including storing your items and bags)
  • An Otafest Volunteer shirt,​
  • A volunteer badge and lanyard,​
  • Special volunteer-exclusive swag, and​
  • An invitation to Otafest's Volunteer Appreciation Day in the summer.

Food

Otafest has historically provided food for volunteers, however volunteer surveys have shown us that volunteers prefer their own freedom of choice when it comes to selecting food.

As such, instead of directly providing food, we offer the following compensation program for volunteers to purchase their own food during their breaks. Volunteers will be compensated based on the number of shifts they are working:

4 Shifts
$30.00

5-6 Shifts
$40.00

7-8 Shifts
$50.00

9+ Shifts
$60.00
+ our undying affection and gratitude

Café, Pokémon League, Sketch Drive, and other specialized volunteers receive $50.00 regardless of the number of shifts worked as these positions tend to require a large commitment prior to Otafest.

Departments

You may select which role(s) for which you are interested in volunteering on your application. Below is a short description of each position, click on each title to expand its details.

Admissions - "The Gatekeeper"

Admissions volunteers are responsible for selling Otafest admission to patrons and checking in those who pre-purchased their tickets online. This position is a fast paced work environment, however you should still be friendly and interactive with all of our attendees!

All Volunteer Responsibilities

  • Completing the online training module.
  • Being on time for your shift(s).
  • Checking in with the volunteer room before your first shift each day.
  • Answering attendee questions or directing them to someone who can help.

 

This Role’s Responsibilities

  • Receiving payment for tickets using both a till and iPad (must input everything accurately).
  • Scanning pre-purchased attendee tickets.
  • Applying the correct wristband to patron wrists after their payment has been processed.

 

Useful Skills

  • Customer service experience is an asset.
  • Cashier experience / money-handling experience (basic math skill is required).
  • Must be able to cope with busy lineups and stay calm under pressure.
  • Must be able to think on your feet and adapt quickly to changing situations.
CONbini - "The Distributor"

CONbini volunteers promote and sell Japanese baked goods, drinks, and other items to hungry/thirsty patrons throughout the weekend.

All Volunteer Responsibilities

  • Completing the online training module.
  • Being on time for your shift(s).
  • Checking in with the volunteer room before your first shift each day.
  • Answering attendee questions or directing them to someone who can help.

 

This Role’s Responsibilities

  • Recommending and selling Japanese baked goods and assorted Japanese drinks to patrons.
  • Learning about the products to assist patrons with any dietary concerns.

 

Useful Skills

  • Cash handling skill is an asset but not required.
  • Customer service and people interaction experience are assets.
  • Having a fun and outgoing personality.
Exhibitor Hall - "The Charitable Guide"

Exhibitor Hall volunteers get attendees to their destinations in the Exhibitor Hall, even if the attendees don’t know where they’re going! These knowledgeable volunteers constantly interact with attendees, whether answering questions, directing them, or encouraging them to donate to our yearly charity.

All Volunteer Responsibilities

  • Completing the online training module.
  • Being on time for your shift(s).
  • Checking in with the volunteer room before your first shift each day.
  • Answering attendee questions or directing them to someone who can help.

 

This Role’s Responsibilities

  • Checking for attendee proof of admission (wristbands) before they can enter the area.
  • Monitoring and promoting traffic flow.
  • Helping attendees find the booths/tables they seek or with other questions they have.
  • Collecting funds for Otafest’s selected charity.

 

Useful Skills

  • Customer service and people interaction experience are assets.
  • Comfort with a certain level of activity (you’ll be on your feet and moving around a lot).
  • Having a friendly/outgoing/energetic/enthusiastic personality.
Gaming - "The Observer"

We’d say the pun was unintended, but would you really believe us?

Gaming volunteers patrol the gaming room area to monitor occupancy, assist attendees with equipment, and ensure that everyone is playing fairly, sharing the equipment fairly, etc.

All Volunteer Responsibilities

  • Completing the online training module.
  • Being on time for your shift(s).
  • Checking in with the volunteer room before your first shift each day.
  • Answering attendee questions or directing them to someone who can help.

 

This Role’s Responsibilities

  • Checking for attendee proof of admission (wristbands) before they can enter the area.
  • Monitoring gaming equipment to ensure that it’s being safely used and shared fairly.
  • Assisting attendees with getting set up on a game and/or any technical difficulties.
  • Monitoring occupancy and foot traffic.
  • Cleaning/organizing the equipment in the room.
  • Setting up and tearing down the room.

 

Useful Skills

  • Customer service and people interaction experience are assets.
  • Being observant and aware of your surroundings.
  • Comfort with large groups of people.
  • Gaming knowledge is an asset but not necessary.
  • Technical troubleshooting knowledge is an asset but not necessary.
Line Control - "The Traffic Cop"

Line Control volunteers are enthusiastic and energetic individuals who manage line flow and attendee traffic all around the festival. They use kindness and fun to keep people flowing. They are able to guide folks to events and are often the first to meet attendees and introduce them to the Otafest experience, so kindness and a positive attitude are mandatory! Line Control Assistants are needed in all areas of the festival. Join this team if you like to be organized and in control!

All Volunteer Responsibilities

  • Completing the online training module.
  • Being on time for your shift(s).
  • Checking in with the volunteer room before your first shift each day.
  • Answering attendee questions or directing them to someone who can help.

 

This Role’s Responsibilities

  • Providing directions to attendees looking for specific events.
  • Directing and forming lines for events and rooms.
  • Ensuring attendees still have a good time, even while they’re waiting in line.
  • Working closely and communicating with Otafest’s Policy Enforcement team.
  • Checking for attendee proof of admission (wristbands).

 

Useful Skills

  • Ability to remain calm and kind under pressure.
  • Customer service and people interaction experience.
  • Good with directions and able to read maps.
  • Team oriented.
  • Solution-oriented mentality.
Merchandise - "The Merchant"

Merchandise volunteers run the Otafest Merchandise booth. This position involves promoting and advertising Otafest merchandise as well as assisting attendees with sizing and information regarding the different products.

All Volunteer Responsibilities

  • Completing the online training module.
  • Being on time for your shift(s).
  • Checking in with the volunteer room before your first shift each day.
  • Answering attendee questions or directing them to someone who can help.

 

This Role’s Responsibilities

  • Selling Otafest merchandise.
  • Assisting patrons with sizing and color recommendations.
  • Accepting transactions in cash and card.

 

Useful Skills

  • Cash handling is an asset.
  • Customer service and people interaction experience are important assets.
  • Sociable and outgoing personality.
Policy Enforcement - "The Bodyguard"

Policy Enforcers maintain a visible presence at the festival, ensure that Otafest’s policies are being followed, and that everyone has a safe and fun time.  If you like being out and about while meeting new people this is the role for you.

All Volunteer Responsibilities

  • Completing the online training module.
  • Being on time for your shift(s).
  • Checking in with the volunteer room before your first shift each day.
  • Answering attendee questions or directing them to someone who can help.

 

This Role’s Responsibilities

  • Ensuring costume props conform to Otafest’s guidelines.
  • Assisting in running the Policy Enforcement Desk.
  • Walking throughout the festival space to maintain a visible presence.
  • Reporting any unsafe situations/behaviors.
  • If you’re above the age of 18, we may ask you to do late-night shifts (e.g. shifts which end at midnight).

 

Useful Skills

  • Customer service experience is an asset.
  • Observant and detail-oriented mind.
  • Energetic and enthusiastic to be at Otafest.
Programming - "The Performer"

Programming Helpers ensure that Otafest events/panels run smoothly and oversee that event transitions are as seamless as possible. These volunteers work with panelists and attendees to create a safe and fun experience for everyone involved. This role is great for those who enjoy being more involved with various Otafest events.

All Volunteer Responsibilities

  • Completing the online training module.
  • Being on time for your shift(s).
  • Checking in with the volunteer room before your first shift each day.
  • Answering attendee questions or directing them to someone who can help.

 

This Role’s Responsibilities

  • Checking for attendee proof of admission (wristbands) before they can enter panel rooms.
    • This is what you will be doing for the majority of the time.
  • Monitoring and supervising assigned events.
  • Assisting Programming staff and Zone Leads with any general help they need.
  • Performing panel setup and tear down (includes some light lifting).
  • Assisting panelists with technical issues and troubleshooting for their event (or finding someone who can help).

 

Useful Skills

  • Knowledge of special equipment handling (speakers, computers, etc.)
  • Personable and friendly.
  • Comfortable with large groups of people.
  • Energetic and enthusiastic to be at Otafest.
  • Observant and aware of your surroundings.

Important Dates

  • Early March 2024 - Volunteer applications close.
  • Mid-Late March 2024 - Volunteers informed of their selection status.
  • Late March 2024 - Confirmations due. This is the deadline to confirm that you still want to volunteer.
  • Mid-April 2024 - Optional in-person volunteer orientation.
  • Late April 2024 - Initial schedule sent to volunteers to be reviewed for conflicts.
  • Early May 2024 - Final, revised schedule sent to volunteers.

Questions & Emails

Otafest communicates primarily through email. Please check your email regularly and add volunteer@otafest.com on your safe senders list as we have had occurrences where our emails were flagged as spam.

If you have any questions or concerns please send us an email to volunteer@otafest.com.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a minimum age to volunteer?

You must be at least 13 years old by the date of your first shift.

Can I volunteer with my friends?

If you’d like to volunteer with your friends, please indicate it on the relevant field in the application form. We do our best to assign you with your friends but we can’t guarantee that all of your shifts will overlap. 

What should I do if I already bought Otafest tickets and I'm accepted as a volunteer?

Once you’ve received your acceptance email, let the volunteer team know at volunteer@otafest.com and your pass will be refunded.

Can I show up early/before my shift to enjoy Otafest?

Yes! Just head to the volunteer room first to check in early and get your materials & wristband.

After that, you’re free to explore Otafest until your shift!

Why is there a fee?

The fee exists because Otafest needs to cover upfront administrative costs that we could otherwise not bear, including your admission to the festival on the days/times that you’re not actively volunteering. We hope that our food compensation program will help offset the fee (and then some) for you.

Can I cancel my application?

If you must cancel, you can email volunteer@otafest.com to let the team know as soon as possible so we can reassign your shifts.

Please note that cancellation after May 1st will negatively affect your opportunity to volunteer for future Otafest events unless there are extenuating circumstances.

What should I bring to my shift?

Bring anything you need to ensure you can have a safe time while volunteering.

  • Comfortable shoes
    • You’ll probably be on your feet a lot!
  • A water bottle
    • There are refill stations around the venue
  • Snacks
    • Especially if you have any dietary considerations
  • Cash/wallet/cards
    • If you want to buy anything from the Exhibitor Hall, etc.
  • Extra clothes (that are not your volunteer shirt)
    • When you’re not volunteering, please don’t wear your shirt or we’ll think you’re still on shift and pull you in to do more work!
